## 1. My Mother at Sixty-Six (Kamala Das)
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **The "Ashy" Metaphor:** Must link to "Corpse" and "Life-less-ness." Any audit failing to mention the pallor of death is incomplete.
*   **Contrast Mechanism:** "Trees sprinting" and "Merry children" must be contextualized as an escape from the "fear" inside the car.
*   **The Familiar Ache:** Childhood fear of loss/separation.

## 2. Keeping Quiet (Pablo Neruda)
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Exotic Moment:** A moment without "rush" or "engines."
*   **Total Inactivity vs. Death:** CRITICAL DISTINCTION. Neruda explicitly rejects death; he advocates for "stillness" which is a life-affirming pause.
*   **The Earth as Teacher:** Winter looks like death but hides life (seeds).
*   **Fishermen & Salt Gatherers:** Symbols of human-nature harmony and self-healing.

### Board-Aligned Triggers:
| Trigger Point | Verbatim Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Interruption of Sadness** | We are sad because we don't understand ourselves; silence provides introspection. |
| **Victory with no survivors** | Futility of war/competition. |

## 3. A Thing of Beauty (John Keats)
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Loveliness Increases:** Contra-Entropy doctrine. Beauty does not decay; it grows.
*   **Bower Quiet:** Physical tranquility leads to spiritual "quiet breathing."
*   **Mighty Dead:** Recognition that human heroism is a "shape of beauty."
*   **Endless Fountain:** Divine origin of beauty (Immortal drink).

### Board-Aligned Triggers:
| Trigger Point | Verbatim Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The Pall** | Dark spirits/despondence removed by the presence of beauty. |
| **Inhuman Dearth** | Critique of human selfishness vs. nature’s generosity. |

## 4. A Roadside Stand (Robert Frost)
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Childish Longing:** The pathetic, futile wait for city cars to stop.
*   **Greedy Good-Doers:** Critique of paternalistic charity that robs the poor of their autonomy.
*   **The Flower of Cities:** City money as the lifeblood of civilization (unshared with the rural poor).
*   **Dark Empathy:** The poet's desire to end the farmers' suffering "at one stroke."

### Board-Aligned Triggers:
| Trigger Point | Verbatim Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Polished Traffic** | Symbol of indifferent urban progress. |
| **Soothe them out of their wits** | Displacement of rural folk under the guise of "betterment." |

## 5. Aunt Jennifer's Tigers (Adrienne Rich)
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Topaz Denizens:** Vibrant, sharp, and fearless.
*   **Massive Weight of Ring:** Symbol of marriage as a "mastery" or "ordeal."
*   **Fluttering Fingers:** Physical manifestation of fear (Parkinsonian or neuro-physical stress).
*   **Survival through Art:** Aunt Jennifer dies, but the tigers (her spirit) prance on.
