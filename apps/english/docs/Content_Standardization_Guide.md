# Content Standardization Guide: English Core Class XII

This document serves as the **Master Blueprint** for content generation in the English Core app. It adapts the quantitative standards of commerce subjects into **Qualitative "Forensics"** and **Thematic Analysis** required for the 2026 examination cycle.

## 1. Literature Forensic Detail & Hierarchy
**Standard**: Content must evolve from "Plot Summary" to "Competency-Based Interpretation".
*   **Structure**: `Theme` (Level 1) → `Character Archetype` (Level 2) → `Narrative Milestone` (Level 3).
*   **Depth**:
    *   **Level 1 (Thematic Pillar)**: Identifies universal truths (e.g., "Linguistic Chauvinism" or "Socio-Economic Entrapment").
    *   **Level 2 (Inference)**: Groups motivations and sub-themes (e.g., "M. Hamel's Repentance" or "Franz's Awakening").
    *   **Level 3 (Points)**: Detailed analytical bullet points explaining "Why" a character acts (e.g., "The steel canister symbolizes the weight of responsibility...").
    *   **Actionable Insight**: Theory must include **Critical Commentary** (e.g., "The author uses a satirical tone to mock royal hubris").

## 2. Linguistic Precision & Lexicon
**Standard**: **Zero Paraphrasing** of NCERT-specific terminology.
*   **Method**: All "Contextual Expressions" highlighted by NCERT must be explicitly included in the audit.
*   **Standard**: Keywords that trigger "Expression" marks (content, fluency, and accuracy) must be bolded.
*   **Example**:
    *   ❌ "Children working hard in factories."
    *   ✅ "**Slog their daylight hours** in illegal glass furnaces."
*   **Requirement**: Include the "Author's Style" (e.g., "Autobiographical," "Lyrical," "Journalistic Reportage") in the header of every chapter.

## 3. Analytical Unit: The Extract (RTC Mastery)
**Standard**: Every prose chapter and poem must be presented as a "Extract-Evidence-Interpretation" unit.
*   **Components**:
    *   **The Extract source**: Verbatim text from NCERT.
    *   **Tone Decoder**: Scientific identification of the narrator's tone (e.g., Sardonical, Melancholic, Resigned).
    *   **Symbolism Check**: Explicitly identifying symbols (e.g., "The Third Level" = Escapism).
    *   **Imagery Map**: Audit of sensory details (Visual, Auditory, Tactile) used in the passage.
*   **Quality Metrics**:
    *   **Extract Accuracy**: 100% verbatim from source text.
    *   **Inferential Depth**: Moving beyond "What happened" to "What is implied."
    *   **Extrapolation**: Designing "What if" scenarios for character response (e.g., "How would M. Hamel react to German music?").

## 4. Flashcard Selection Strategy (Forensic Focus)
**Standard**: Build active recall for **inference and literary devices**.
*   **Quantity**: Minimum 40+ cards per chapter.
*   **Categories**:
    *   **Vocabulary**: "Meanings of high-yield NCERT phrases."
    *   **Literary Devices**: "Identify the device in: 'smiling, smiling, and smiling'."
    *   **Symbolism**: "What does the ironmaster's invitation represent?"
    *   **Character Traits**: "Evidence of Mukesh's optimism?"
    *   **Tone Identification**: "Tone of the blacksmith's comment to Franz?"

## 5. Writing Skill standardization (The "Format Authority")
**Standard**: **Verbatim CBSE Latest Layouts**.
*   **Blueprint**:
    *   **Fixed Format**: Header, Box, Salutation, and Sign-off rules.
    *   **Content Skeletons**: Pre-built "Problem-Cause-Solution" or "Opening-Body-Purpose" matrices.
    *   **Vocabulary Upgrades**: Replacing common verbs with high-impact reporting verbs (e.g., "states" vs. "propounds", "say" vs. "emphasize").
    *   **Checklist**: Mandatory 5-point checklist for every format (e.g., "Is the Notice in a box?", "Is the date on the left?").

## 6. Poetry Section Standard (The "Stanza Audit")
**Standard**: **Line-by-Line "Reference to Context" (RTC)**.
*   **Requirement**: Every poem must break down:
    1.  **Stanza interpretation**: Practical meaning.
    2.  **Poetic Device Map**: Simile, Metaphor, Personification, Irony, etc.
    3.  **Speaker's Persona**: Detailed profile of the poetic voice.
    4.  **Emotional Arc**: How the mood shifts from stanza 1 to the end.

## 7. AI Explanation: "The Critical Voice"
**Standard**: Bridge the gap between the student's surface reading and the examiner's marking scheme.
*   **Pedagogy**:
    *   **The "Why"**: Don't just give the answer; explain the character's motivation.
    *   **Drafting in Persona**: Show how to write as the character (e.g., "If I were Aunt Jennifer, I would feel...").
    *   **Comparative Logic**: Explain why a character in Chapter A is similar/different to a character in Chapter B.

## 8. SQP Mapping (The "Pattern" Law)
**Standard**: Every question must be tagged with **"Trigger Types"**.
*   **Persona Trigger**: Questions requiring a diary, letter, or speech in character.
*   **Comparative Trigger**: Questions requiring the merging of two themes.
*   **Data Accuracy**: 100% verbatim from official 2023-2026 Sample Papers.
