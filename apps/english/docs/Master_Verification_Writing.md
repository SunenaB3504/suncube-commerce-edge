# MASTER VERIFICATION REPORT: WRITING AUTHORITY (Class XII)
**Scope:** Notices, Invitations & Replies, Formal Letters (Editor/Job), Articles & Reports.
**Metric:** Compliance with CBSE Marking Scheme (2024-2026) – Format, Content, and Expression.

---

## 1. Notice Writing
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Format Integrity:** Box (Border) is mandatory. Organization name, NOTICE, Date, Heading must be clear.
*   **The 5 Ws:** What (Event), When (Date/Time), Where (Venue), Who (Eligibility), Whom (Contact).
*   **Word Limit:** Strict adherence to 50 words.

### Scoring Rubric:
| Aspect | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Format** | Name of school, NOTICE, Date, Heading, Writer's name & designation. |
| **Content** | Purpose of notice, event date/time/venue/contact details. |
| **Expression** | Grammatical accuracy, formal tone, and coherence. |

---

## 2. Invitations & Replies
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Formal Invitation (Card):** Third-person, no signature. RSVP at bottom left. Phone/Address at bottom left.
*   **Formal Invitation (Letter):** Standard formal letter format. Used for specific guests/dignitaries.
*   **Informal Invitation:** First-person, emotional warmth, standard informal layout.
*   **Replies:** Formal replies must stay in the third person ("Mr. X thanks Mr. Y for the invitation...").

---

## 3. Formal Letters
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Letter to Editor:** Focus on social/civic issues. Do not ask the Editor to solve the problem; ask them to publish so authorities take note.
*   **Job Application:** Cover Letter + Bio-Data (CV). Bio-Data must have clear sections for Education, Experience, and References.

### Vocabulary Triggers:
*   *Instead of "problem", use "menace" or "grievance".*
*   *Instead of "look into", use "solicit prompt intervention".*

---

## 4. Long Composition (Article & Report)
### Forensic Anchors:
*   **Article:** Title (Catchy) + By-line. Systematic flow: Intro -> Causes/Effects -> Conclusion/Suggestions.
*   **Report:** Headline + By-line + Date/Place (for Newspapers) or just Heading/By-line (for School Magazine). Past tense is essential for reports.

### Expression Markers:
*   **Articles:** Analytical, persuasive, and informative.
*   **Reports:** Factual, objective, and chronological.
