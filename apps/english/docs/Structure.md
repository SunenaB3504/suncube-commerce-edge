# English App Content Structure Plan

Based on a review of the latest SQPs and the high-yield pedagogy found in `Exam-Capsule.md`, the English app should move away from pure plot summaries and focus on **Competency-Based Comprehension**.

## 1. Literature (Section C) - "Analytical Depth"
For each Prose chapter in Flamingo and Vistas, follow the **Forensics-based model**:
- **The Core Narrative (Gist)**: 3-sentence summary (Inciting Incident, Climax, Resolution).
- **Forensics for Extracts**:
  - **Tone Decoder**: Identifying the specific narrative tone (e.g., Satirical, Melancholic, Ambiguous) with textual evidence.
  - **Literary Device Bank**: Identifying metaphors, irony, and symbolism used in the text.
  - **Symbolism Check**: Explaining the deeper meaning of objects (e.g., The Steel Canister, The Wooden Toy Tiger).
- **Question Banks**:
  - **Keyword Cheat Sheet**: High-scoring words that examiners look for (e.g., "Linguistic Imperialism", "Waking-dream wish fulfillment").
  - **Sensory Details**: Audit of visual/auditory/tactile details from the chapter for 2-mark descriptive questions.
  - **Thematic Blueprints**: 5-mark answer frameworks focusing on "Thematic Thesis" rather than plot.
  - **Extrapolation Points**: Cross-chapter comparisons (e.g., Comparing Dr. Sadao's ethics to Edla's compassion).
- **Error Mitigation**:
  - **Spelling Traps**: Correct spelling of difficult names and terminology.
  - **Common Misconceptions**: Clarifying persistent misunderstandings (e.g., Why the Tiger King died).

### **Poetry Section Structure** (Flamingo):
- **Core Stanza Analysis**: Line-by-line interpretation for Reference to Context (RTC) questions.
- **Poetic Device Map**: Identifying devices like Simile, Metaphor, Personification, etc., with verbatim line references.
- **Thematic Core & Message**: The universal message of the poem (e.g., "Quietude as a tool for self-introspection" for *Keeping Quiet*).
- **Speaker's Persona**: Emotional state and perspective of the poetic voice.
- **Keywords for RTC Mastery**: Specific vocabulary for stanza-based inference questions.

## 2. Writing Skills (Section B) - "Format & Flow"
- **Verbatim Formats**: Latest CBSE formats for Notices, Invitations, and Letters.
- **Value Point Matrix**: For Article/Report writing, provide a matrix of "Problem-Cause-Solution" points for trending topics.
- **Expression Scoring**: Tips on how to improve 'Expression' and 'Fluency' scores through vocabulary.

## 3. Reading Skills (Section A) - "Inference & Title"
- **The "Title" Strategy**: Rules for choosing the best title (Core Theme vs. Catchy Hook).
- **Tone Identification Hub**: A cheat sheet of commonly used words for "Tone" (e.g., Skeptical, Commendatory, Objective).
- **Vocabulary Boosters**: Meaning and usage of complex words found in past year passages.

## 4. Exam Strategy (The "Examiner's Lens")
- **Quoting Guidelines**: How and when to use direct quotes effectively.
- **Time Management**: Section-wise time allocation.
- **Word Limit Mastery**: Writing punchy answers that stay within the 40-50/120-150 word limits.
