⚡ Exam Morning Review: Chapter-by-Chapter Guide ⚡

Instructions: Review chapter by chapter. Focus on the Yellow "Archive Special" boxes.

📖 Book 1, Chapter 1: Accounting for Partnership (Basic Concepts)

🧠 Core Formulas

  • Interest on Loan: Principal × Rate/100 × Months/12. (If silent: 6% per annum).
  • Manager's Commission:
    • Before Charging: Net Profit × (Rate / 100).
    • After Charging: Net Profit × (Rate / 100 + Rate).
  • Interest on Drawings (Average Period Method):
    Formula: Total Drawings × (Rate/100) × (Average Period / 12)
Frequency Beginning Middle End
Monthly 6.5 Months 6 Months 5.5 Months
Quarterly 7.5 Months 6 Months 4.5 Months
Half-Yearly 3.5 Months 3 Months 2.5 Months

⚠️ Archive Special (Adjustments)

  • Past Adjustment (Single Entry): Gaining Partner Debit (Net Debit) to Sacrificing Partner (Net Credit).
  • Guarantee Deficiency: Distribute deficiency among guaranteeing partners in Agreed Ratio (or Old Profit Ratio if silent).

📝 Fixed vs Fluctuating Capital

  • Fixed Capital: Capital Account stays fixed. All adjustments (IOC, Salary, Drawings, Profit Share) go to Current Account.
  • Fluctuating Capital: Capital Account absorbs everything — no separate Current Account.
  • P&L Appropriation A/c Structure:
    Dr side: IOC + Salary + Commission + Partner's Share of Profit.
    Cr side: Net Profit (from P&L) + IOD received from partners.
  • Insufficient Profit Rule for IOC: If Net Profit + IOD < Total IOC → Pay IOC proportionately. IOC NOT paid in full if profit is insufficient.

📝 P&L Appropriation Journal Entries

  • Transfer of Net Profit: Profit & Loss A/c Dr. To P&L Appropriation A/c
  • Interest on Capital:
    1. Interest on Capital A/c Dr. To Partner's Capital/Current A/cs
    2. P&L Appropriation A/c Dr. To Interest on Capital A/c
  • Interest on Drawings:
    1. Partner's Capital/Current A/cs Dr. To Interest on Drawings A/c
    2. Interest on Drawings A/c Dr. To P&L Appropriation A/c
  • Partner's Salary/Commission:
    1. Salary/Commission A/c Dr. To Partner's Capital/Current A/cs
    2. P&L Appropriation A/c Dr. To Salary/Commission A/c
  • Share of Profit: P&L Appropriation A/c Dr. To Partner's Capital/Current A/cs
  • Share of Loss: Partner's Capital/Current A/cs Dr. To P&L Appropriation A/c

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P1

  • "Rate not given" → default 6% for both Interest on Loan AND IOD.
  • Spot Fixed vs Fluctuating: If question shows TWO columns per partner (Capital + Current) → Fixed. ONE column → Fluctuating.
  • Guarantee question: First distribute profit normally, then fix the shortfall. The guaranteeing partner(s) bear the deficit — subtract from their share.
  • Past Adjustment trick: Prepare a table → Row = each partner, Column = Old ratio credit vs New ratio debit → Net difference = single adjustment entry.
  • IOD trap: "Equal drawings at end of each month" → 5.5 months. Beginning of each month → 6.5 months. Do NOT confuse with quarterly.

📖 Book 1, Chapter 1 (Cont'd): Goodwill Valuation

🧠 Core Formulas

  • Average Profit Method: Average Profit × No. of Years Purchase.
  • Weighted Average: Sum(Profit × Weight) / Sum of Weights.
  • Super Profit Method:
    1. Normal Profit = Capital Employed × (Normal Rate of Return / 100).
    2. Super Profit = Actual Average Profit - Normal Profit.
    3. Goodwill = Super Profit × No. of Years Purchase.
  • Capitalisation Method:
    • Of Average Profit: (Average Profit × 100/NRR) - Capital Employed.
    • Of Super Profit: Super Profit × (100/Normal Rate of Return).

⚠️ Archive Special (Reserves)

  • Workmen Compensation Reserve: If Claim > Reserve → Debit Excess to Revaluation Account.
  • Investment Fluctuation Reserve: If Fall in Value > Reserve → Debit Excess to Revaluation Account.

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P2

  • "Capitalisation of Super Profit" shortcut: Super Profit ÷ NRR% = Goodwill. No need for intermediate steps.
  • Hidden Goodwill trigger: Question says "admitted for 1/4 share for ₹X capital" — always check if implied firm value = X × 4 matches total actual capital. If not → Hidden Goodwill exists.
  • Weighted Average vs Simple Average: If question gives recent years higher weights → Use Weighted. If all years equal → Simple Average.
  • WCR/IFR rule: Reserve = ₹5k, Claim = ₹8k → Excess ₹3k to Revaluation (Loss). Reserve = ₹5k, Claim = ₹3k → ₹2k surplus transferred to Partners (Gain to Old Partners in Old Ratio).

📖 Book 1, Chapter 2: Admission of a Partner

🧠 Core Formulas

  • Sacrificing Ratio: Old Share - New Share. (Used for Goodwill Premium).
  • Hidden Goodwill:
    Hidden Goodwill = Total Capital of New Firm - Actual Combined Capital
    Total Capital = New Partner's Capital × Reciprocal of Share.
    Actual Capital = Old Partners (Adjusted) + New Partner's Capital.

📝 Admission of a Partner Journal Entries

  • Premium for Goodwill brought in Cash:
    1. Bank A/c Dr. To Premium for Goodwill A/c
    2. Premium for Goodwill A/c Dr. To Sacrificing Partners' Capital A/cs
  • Goodwill already appearing in Books:
    Old Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Goodwill A/c (In Old Ratio)
  • Premium NOT brought in Cash:
    New Partner's Current A/c Dr. To Sacrificing Partners' Capital A/cs
  • Adjustment for Accumulated Profits/Losses:
    Profits: General Reserve/P&L A/c Dr. To Old Partners' Capital A/cs (In Old Ratio)
    Losses: Old Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Profit & Loss A/c (In Old Ratio)
  • Revaluation of Assets & Liabilities:
    Gain (Asset ↑, Liability ↓, Unrecorded Asset): Asset/Liability A/c Dr. To Revaluation A/c
    Loss (Asset ↓, Liability ↑, Unrecorded Liability): Revaluation A/c Dr. To Asset/Liability A/c
    Transfer Profit: Revaluation A/c Dr. To Old Partners' Capital A/cs (In Old Ratio)
  • Adjustment of Capitals:
    Excess Withdrawn: Old Partner's Capital A/c Dr. To Cash/Bank A/c
    Deficiency Brought In: Cash/Bank A/c Dr. To Old Partner's Capital A/c

⚠️ Archive Special (Revaluation)

  • Bad Debts Recovered: Credit Revaluation (Gain).
  • "Provision increased BY 2k": Debit Revaluation 2k.
  • "Provision increased TO 5k": Debit Revaluation by (5k - Old Provision).
  • "Asset overvalued BY 25%": Book Value = 125% of True Value → True = BV ÷ 1.25. "Undervalued BY 20%" → BV = 80% of True → True = BV ÷ 0.80. Always divide, never subtract, for "BY" questions.

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P3

  • New ratio given directly? → Sacrificing = Old share − New share per partner. Negative result = that partner gains (unusual but possible).
  • "Equal sacrifice" or "in old ratio" → same thing. Sacrificing ratio = Old PSR.
  • Premium NOT brought in cash: Credit goes to sacrificing partners' Capital/Current. The word "privately" or "personally" does NOT apply here — that means goodwill adjustments outside firm books.
  • "Provision TO ₹X" → Calculate difference from existing provision. "Provision BY ₹X" → Use ₹X directly. This is the most common mistake in revaluation.
  • New partner's capital adjustment: After goodwill, revaluation, reserves — check if old partners need to bring in or withdraw capital to match target capitals.

📖 Book 1, Chapter 3: Retirement and Death of a Partner

🧠 Core Formulas

  • Gaining Ratio: New Share - Old Share. (Used for Goodwill Adjustment).
  • Profit Calculation (Death):
    • Time Basis: Previous Year Profit × (Months Alive / 12) × Share.
    • Turnover Basis: (Previous Profit / Previous Sales) × Sales till Death × Share.
  • Executor's Loan Interest: 6% per annum (if no rate agreed).

📝 Retirement/Death of a Partner Journal Entries

  • Treatment of Goodwill (No Goodwill Account):
    Gaining Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Retiring/Deceased Partner's Capital A/c
  • Goodwill existing in Books (Written off):
    All Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Goodwill A/c (In Old Ratio)
  • Adjustment for Accumulated Profits/Losses:
    Profits: Reserves/P&L A/c Dr. To All Partners' Capital A/cs (In Old Ratio)
    Losses: All Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Profit & Loss A/c (In Old Ratio)
  • Revaluation of Assets & Liabilities:
    Gain: Asset/Liability A/c Dr. To Revaluation A/c
    Loss: Revaluation A/c Dr. To Asset/Liability A/c
    Transfer Profit: Revaluation A/c Dr. To All Partners' Capital A/cs (In Old Ratio)
  • Share of Profit (Middle of the Year):
    1. Create Suspense: Profit & Loss Suspense A/c Dr. To Retiring/Deceased Partner's Capital A/c
    2. Close Suspense: Gaining Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Profit & Loss Suspense A/c
  • Disposal of Amount Due (Settlement):
    Paid in Cash full: Retiring Partner's Capital A/c Dr. To Cash/Bank A/c
    Transferred to Loan: Retiring Partner's Capital A/c Dr. To Retiring Partner's Loan A/c
    Interest Due on Loan: Interest A/c Dr. To Retiring Partner's Loan A/c
    Loan Instalment Paid: Retiring Partner's Loan A/c Dr. To Cash/Bank A/c

⚠️ Archive Special (Section 37)

  • Outgoing partner can claim:
    Option A: Interest @ 6% per annum on unpaid balance.
    Option B: Share of Profit earned using unpaid capital.
    (Choose whichever is Higher).

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P4

  • Death question? → Step 1: Check if profit calculation method is given (Time or Turnover basis). Step 2: Debit P&L Suspense, Credit Deceased's Capital. Step 3: Prepare Executor's A/c.
  • Gaining Ratio: If new ratio not given → remaining partners continue in OLD ratio → Gaining Ratio = Old Ratio of remaining partners.
  • Goodwill entry for retirement: ALWAYS Dr Gaining Partners, Cr Retiring Partner — even if goodwill is not in the balance sheet.
  • Section 37 trigger words: "amount due not paid on time" / "settlement deferred" / "instalments" → Calculate both 6% interest AND share of profit → credit whichever is higher to Executor A/c.
  • Balance Sheet after retirement: Remaining partners may need to bring in cash if firm can't pay retiring partner fully → Check available bank balance.

📖 Book 1, Chapter 4: Dissolution of Partnership Firm

🧠 Settlement Rules

  • Section 48 (Order of Payment): 1. Third Party Debts → 2. Partner's Loan → 3. Partner's Capital.
  • Realisation Expenses:
    • Firm pays, Firm bears: Realisation Debit to Bank.
    • Firm pays, Partner bears: Partner Capital Debit to Bank.
    • Partner pays, Firm bears: Realisation Debit to Partner Capital.

📝 Dissolution of Firm Journal Entries

  • Transfer of Assets (at book value):
    Realisation A/c Dr. To Assets (Individually) A/c
  • Transfer of Liabilities (External):
    Liabilities (Individually) Dr. To Realisation A/c
  • Realisation of Assets (Cash/Bank):
    Bank A/c Dr. To Realisation A/c
  • Asset taken over by a Partner:
    Partner's Capital A/c Dr. To Realisation A/c
  • Payment of Liabilities & Realisation Expenses:
    Realisation A/c Dr. To Bank A/c
  • Liability assumed by a Partner:
    Realisation A/c Dr. To Partner's Capital A/c
  • Realisation Expenses paid by Partner on behalf of Firm:
    Realisation A/c Dr. To Partner's Capital A/c
  • Transfer of Profit/Loss on Realisation:
    Profit: Realisation A/c Dr. To Partners' Capital A/cs
    Loss: Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Realisation A/c
  • Transfer of General Reserve/Accumulated Profits:
    General Reserve A/c Dr. To Partners' Capital A/cs (In Profit Sharing Ratio)
  • Payment of Partner's Loan:
    Partner's Loan A/c Dr. To Bank A/c
  • Final Settlement of Partners' Accounts:
    Deficiency Brought In: Bank A/c Dr. To Partner's Capital A/c
    Surplus Paid Out: Partners' Capital A/cs Dr. To Bank A/c

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P5

  • "Asset taken by creditor" → STOP. No entry. This is the #1 trap. No Realisation entry, no Bank entry. Both accounts close silently.
  • Intangible assets (Goodwill, Patents): If question says "not taken over" or is silent → realised at NIL. Only realise if a specific amount is stated.
  • Tangible fixed assets: Silence → realised at Book Value (no profit/loss in Realisation A/c).
  • Partner's Loan vs Firm's Liability: Partner's Loan → paid separately after outside creditors, BEFORE capital. NOT through Realisation A/c.
  • Realisation Profit/Loss check: Cr side (assets realised + liab taken over by partner) MINUS Dr side (assets BV + liab paid + expenses) = Profit if positive.

⚠️ Archive Special (No Entry Cases)

  • Asset taken by Creditor: No Entry (Full or Part settlement).
  • Goodwill: If balance sheet Goodwill is realised → Bank to Realisation. If not realised → NO ENTRY (Assume 0).
  • Tangible Asset: If silent about realisation → Assume Book Value realized.

⚠️ Section 48 — Order of Payment on Dissolution

  • Mandatory sequence (must memorise):
    1st: Third-party debts (Creditors, Bank Loan, Outsider's Loan e.g. "Ruby's Loan").
    2nd: Partners' loans/advances to firms (e.g. "Hemant's Loan") — rateably.
    3rd: Partners' capital — rateably.
    4th: Surplus (residue) distributed in Profit Sharing Ratio.
  • Key distinction: "Partner's Loan" = Step 2 (advance). "Outsider's Loan using partner's name" = Step 1 (third-party). Check whose loan it actually is.

📖 Book 2, Chapter 1: Accounting for Share Capital (Issue)

🧠 Core Formulas

  • Issue of Shares for Consideration other than Cash:
    No. of Shares = Purchase Consideration / Issue Price
    (Issue Price = Face Value + Premium)
  • Calls in Arrears: Interest @ 10% per annum (Table F). (Usually ignored in exams).
  • Calls in Advance: Interest @ 12% per annum (Table F).

📝 Key Journal Entries

  • Issue of Shares at Par:
    1. Application: Bank A/c Dr. To Share Application A/c
    2. Transfer: Share Application A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c
    3. Allotment Due: Share Allotment A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c
    4. Allotment Received: Bank A/c Dr. To Share Allotment A/c
  • Issue of Shares at Premium:
    Share Allotment A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c To Securities Premium Reserve A/c
  • Calls-in-Arrears & Calls-in-Advance:
    Arrears (Not Paid): Bank A/c Dr., Calls-in-Arrears A/c Dr. To Share Allotment/Call A/c
    Advance (Paid Early): Bank A/c Dr. To Calls-in-Advance A/c
  • Vendor Issue (Consideration Other Than Cash):
    1. Sundry Assets A/c Dr. To Vendor A/c (Purchase of Asset)
    2. Vendor A/c Dr. To Share Capital A/c (and To Securities Premium Reserve A/c if at premium)

⚠️ Archive Special (Section 52(2) Uses)

  • Securities Premium can ONLY be used for: 1. Bonus Shares (Fully paid). 2. Writing off Preliminary Expenses. 3. Writing off Share Issue Expenses/Commission. 4. Premium on Redemption of Debentures/Preference Shares. 5. Buy-back of shares.
  • Minimum Subscription (90% Rule): If applications received < 90% of issued shares → Company CANNOT proceed to allotment → Refund ALL application money.
  • Premium on Redemption of Preference Shares:
    Preference Share Capital Dr + Premium on Redemption Dr To Preference Shareholders.
    Premium source: Securities Premium Reserve (first) or P&L (residual).

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P6

  • Shares issued to Vendor: No bank in the entry. Asset Dr → Vendor Cr. Then Vendor Dr → Share Capital (+ Securities Premium) Cr.
  • "Issued at par, redeemable at premium": Debit Loss on Issue = Premium % of face value. Credit Premium on Redemption A/c (liability).
  • Securities Premium trap: Only the 5 permitted uses. If question says "written off against goodwill" → That is NOT permitted under Sec 52(2).
  • Calls in Advance vs Calls in Arrears: Advance = money received early (credited to CA account, earns 12%). Arrears = money not received (deducted from Calls A/c, charged 10% if Table F applies).

📖 Book 2, Chapter 1 (Cont'd): Accounting for Share Capital (Forfeiture)

🧠 Core Formulas

  • Amount Forfeited: Amount actually received (excluding Securities Premium).
  • Capital Reserve (on Reissue):
    Capital Reserve = (Gain per share - Discount per share) × No. of Reissued Shares
    Gain per share = Amount Forfeited / Total Forfeited Shares (Unitary Method).
  • Max Permissible Discount: Amount Forfeited on those specific shares.

📝 Key Journal Entries

  • Forfeiture (Shares originally issued at Par):
    Share Capital A/c Dr. (Called-up Amount)
    To Share Forfeiture A/c (Amount Received)
    To Calls-in-Arrears A/c (Amount Unpaid)
  • Forfeiture (Shares issued at Premium - Premium NOT received):
    Share Capital A/c Dr. (Called-up Amount)
    Securities Premium Reserve A/c Dr. (Unpaid Premium)
    To Share Forfeiture A/c (Amount Received excluding premium)
    To Calls-in-Arrears A/c (Total Unpaid including premium)
  • Reissue of Forfeited Shares:
    Bank A/c Dr. (Amount Received)
    Share Forfeiture A/c Dr. (Discount Allowed)
    To Share Capital A/c (Paid-up Value)
  • Transfer to Capital Reserve:
    Share Forfeiture A/c Dr. To Capital Reserve A/c (Net Gain on Reissued Shares)

⚠️ Archive Special (Pro-rata Calculation)

  • If Pro-rata and Calls in Arrears: 1. Calculate Applied Shares for the defaulter. 2. Find Excess money sent on Application. 3. Deduct Excess from Allotment Due. 4. Net Arrears = Due - Excess. (Do NOT simply use Share × Allotment Money).

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P7

  • Pro-rata question shortcut: If 2,000 shares applied for 1,000 allotted → ratio 2:1. Shares applied by defaulter = Allotted × 2. Excess on application = (Applied − Allotted) × App money per share.
  • "Only allotment money not paid" → No excess from application (applied = allotted). Arrears = allotment money only.
  • Capital Reserve = Forfeited amount on reissued shares ONLY — not on all forfeited shares. If 50 forfeited, 30 reissued → only 30 shares' forfeiture amount goes to CR.
  • Securities Premium forfeited? Only if premium was NOT paid AND forfeiture entry debits SP A/c. If premium was already paid, SP A/c stays; only arrears are forfeited.

📖 Book 2, Chapter 2: Issue and Redemption of Debentures

🧠 Debentures Core Formulas

  • No. of Debentures Issued: Purchase Consideration / Issue Price.
  • Total Loss on Issue: Discount on Issue + Premium upon Redemption.
  • Interest on Debentures: Face Value × (Rate / 100) × (Months / 12).

📝 Key Journal Entries

  • Issue of Debentures for Cash (At Par):
    Bank A/c Dr. To Debenture Application & Allotment A/c
    Debenture Application & Allotment A/c Dr. To ...% Debentures A/c
  • Issue of Debentures for Consideration Other Than Cash:
    Purchase Assets: Sundry Assets A/c Dr. To Vendor A/c
    Issue Debentures: Vendor A/c Dr., Discount on Issue of Debentures A/c Dr. To ...% Debentures A/c, To Securities Premium Reserve A/c
  • Issue of Debentures as Collateral Security:
    Method 1: No entry for debentures. (Only loan entry: Bank A/c Dr. To Bank Loan A/c)
    Method 2: Debenture Suspense A/c Dr. To ...% Debentures A/c
  • Issued at Discount, Redeemable at Premium:
    Bank A/c Dr.
    Loss on Issue of Debentures A/c Dr. (Discount on Issue + Premium on Redemption)
    To ...% Debenture Application & Allotment A/c
    To Premium on Redemption of Debentures A/c
  • Debenture Interest:
    Debenture Interest A/c Dr. To Debentureholders A/c To TDS Payable A/c (if applicable)
    Debentureholders A/c Dr. To Bank A/c

⚠️ Archive Special (Writing Off Loss)

  • Priority: 1. Securities Premium Reserve. 2. Statement of Profit and Loss.
  • Collateral Security: Method 2: Debenture Suspense Debit to Debentures Account.

📝 Debenture Redemption Entries

  • Redemption at Par: Debentures Dr To Debentureholders → Debentureholders Dr To Bank.
  • Redemption at Premium: Debentures Dr + Premium on Redemption Dr To Debentureholders.
  • Open Market Purchase (Own Debentures):
    1. Own Debentures A/c Dr To Bank (at purchase price).
    2. Debentures Dr To Own Debentures + Profit on Cancellation → Capital Reserve.
  • Conversion into Shares: Debentures Dr To Share Capital (+ Securities Premium if issued at premium).

📖 Book 2, Chapter 4: Analysis of Financial Statements

  • Comparative Statement:
    Absolute Change = Current Year − Previous Year.
    % Change = (Absolute Change / Previous Year) × 100.
    Note: If Previous Year figure is 0 or negative, % change = "Not Applicable".
  • Common Size Balance Sheet: Base = Total Assets (= Total Equity & Liabilities) = 100%.
    Each item % = (Item / Total Assets) × 100.
  • Common Size P&L: Base = Revenue from Operations = 100%.
    Each item % = (Item / Revenue from Operations) × 100.
  • Change in Inventories (P&L line item): = Opening (WIP + Finished Goods + Stock-in-Trade) − Closing (WIP + FG + SIT).
    Raw Materials are EXCLUDED — they flow through "Cost of Materials Consumed". Negative result (closing > opening) shown in brackets = credit to P&L.
  • Premium on Redemption — Classification:
    Redeemable >12 months from BS date → Non-Current Liabilities (Long-term Provisions).
    Redeemable ≤12 months → Current Liabilities (Short-term Provisions).
  • Disclosure — Loss on Issue of Debentures (written off from P&L) → shown under Finance Cost in the P&L Statement.
  • Disclosure — Purchase of Raw Materials → Notes to Accounts under heading "Cost of Material Consumed" (NOT a direct line in P&L).

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P8

  • Debenture interest is ALWAYS on Face Value — not on issue price. Even if issued at discount, interest = FV × rate.
  • "Collateral Security" in question: If Method 2 asked → Dr Debenture Suspense, Cr Debentures. When loan repaid → reverse the entry.
  • Comparative Statement trap: If previous year = 0 or negative → write "Not Applicable" in % column. Never divide by zero.
  • Common Size BS: Verify your totals: Current Assets % + Non-Current Assets % = 100%. If not, recheck item allocations.
  • Schedule III classification: "Loose Tools" and "Stores & Spares" → Inventories (Current Assets) but excluded from Quick Assets in ratios.
  • GP Ratio vs NP Ratio: GP Ratio is NOT always higher than NP Ratio. If Indirect Incomes (e.g. interest received, tax refund) exceed Indirect Expenses, NP can be HIGHER than GP. Assertion-Reason trap: "GP is always > NP" is FALSE.

📖 Book 2, Chapter 5: Accounting Ratios

Ratio Formula Ideal
Current Ratio Current Assets / Current Liabilities 2:1
Quick (Liquid) Ratio (Current Assets - Inventory - Prepaid) / Current Liabilities 1:1
Debt-Equity Ratio Debt (Long-term) / Equity (Shareholders' Funds) 2:1
Total Asset to Debt Total Assets / Long-term Debt -
Proprietary Ratio Shareholders' Funds / Total Assets -
Interest Coverage PBIT / Interest on Long-term Debt High
Inventory Turnover Cost of Goods Sold / Average Inventory High

Trade Payables Turnover Net Credit Purchase / Average Trade Payables -

Gross Profit Ratio (Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100 -

Net Profit Ratio (Net Profit / Revenue) × 100 High
ROI (ROCE) PBIT / Capital Employed × 100 High
Debt to Capital Employed Long-term Debt / Capital Employed Low

⚠️ Archive Special (Hidden Formulas)

  • Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): Opening Stock + Purchases + Direct Expenses - Closing Stock.
  • Operating Expenses: Employee Benefit + Depreciation + Other Expenses (Administrative/Selling).
  • Collection Period: 365 / Trade Receivables Ratio.
  • Capital Employed: (Shareholders' Funds + Long term Debt) OR (Non-Current Assets + Working Capital).

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P9

  • "State with reasons whether ratio will increase/decrease/no change": Always check numerator and denominator SEPARATELY. Write: "Since [item] increases/decreases the numerator/denominator, the ratio will [increase/decrease]."
  • Ratio < 1 trap (e.g. Quick Ratio = 0.8): If you subtract the same amount from both numerator and denominator when ratio < 1, the ratio DECREASES. If ratio > 1, it increases. Memorise this rule for "Outstanding Expenses paid" type questions.
  • Quick Ratio can NEVER exceed Current Ratio — Quick Assets are always a subset of Current Assets (Inventory and Prepaid excluded).
  • GP 25% on cost → GP 20% on sales: Convert — if GP = 1/4 of cost, then GP = 1/5 of (cost+GP) = 1/5 of sales. Use this to jump from COGS to Sales directly.
  • Capital Employed = Shareholders' Funds + Long-term Debt = Non-Current Assets + Working Capital. Use whichever form is easier with given data.

🧠 Ultimate Ratio Memory Tricks

  • The 4 Categories (LSAP): Liquidity (Short-term), Solvency (Long-term), Activity (Speed), Profitability (Margins).
  • Name = Formula (Solvency): Read the name, that's the formula! E.g., Debt to Equity = Debt / Equity. Total Assets to Debt = Total Assets / Debt.
  • The Revenue Base Rule (Profitability): Except ROI, EVERY profitability ratio has Revenue from Operations (Net Sales) as the denominator. Just put the ratio name on top! (e.g., Gross Profit / Revenue).
  • Turnover = Average Rule (Activity): Revenue/Cost goes on top, and Average balances go on the bottom. (Exception: Working Capital Turnover).
  • The Quick Subtraction Trick (Liquidity): Quick Assets = Current Assets MINUS Inventory (takes time to sell) MINUS Prepaid Expenses (can't ask for cash back).
  • The Format Cheat Code: Solution is a Pure Ratio (2:1) = Liquidity/Solvency. Solution is in Times (5 times) = Activity or Interest Coverage. Solution is a % = Profitability.

📖 Book 2, Chapter 6: Cash Flow Statement

🧠 Core Format Formulas

  • Net Profit Before Tax (Working Note):
    Surplus (Closing - Opening)
    + Transfer to Reserve
    + Proposed Dividend (Previous Year)
    + Interim Dividend Paid
    + Provision for Tax (Current Year)
  • Operating Profit before Working Capital:
    Net Profit Before Tax + Non-Cash Expenses (Depreciation, Goodwill amortised)
    + Non-Operating Loss (Loss on sale of Fixed Assets)
    - Non-Operating Income (Interest Received, Profit on sale)
  • Tax Paid: Opening Provision + Made - Closing Provision.

⚠️ Archive Special (Treatment)

  • Proposed Dividend: Previous Year = Paid & Provided. Current Year = Ignored.
  • Bank Overdraft: Financing Activity (Not Cash Equivalent).
  • Marketable Securities: Cash & Cash Equivalent.
  • T-Account for Fixed Assets (Investing Activity):
    Dr side: Opening Balance + Purchases (balancing figure).
    Cr side: Cost of Asset Sold + Depreciation for year + Closing Balance.
    Proceeds from Sale (Cash) = Cost of Asset Sold − Accumulated Depreciation ± Profit/Loss on Sale.
  • T-Account for Investments:
    Dr side: Opening Balance + Purchases (balancing figure) + Dividend Received (if income included in cost).
    Cr side: Cost of Investment Sold + Closing Balance.
    Gain on Sale = Proceeds − Cost Sold → shown as Non-operating income; subtract from Operating, add to Investing.

🎯 Question Analysis Tricks — P10

  • "Identify activity" questions: Operating = core business cash flows. Investing = buy/sell long-term assets. Financing = raise or repay capital. Interest paid/received and Dividend received = Operating (for companies under AS-3).
  • Depreciation trap: Always ADD BACK to NPBT in the indirect method. It is a non-cash deduction. Same for amortisation of goodwill, loss on sale of assets.
  • Increase in Current Asset → subtract from Operating (uses cash). Decrease in CA → add back. Increase in CL → add (source). Decrease in CL → subtract.
  • T-account for machinery: If question gives Opening + Closing values and says "depreciation charged" → always set up Dr/Cr T-account to find the unknown (purchase or sale amount).
  • "Net increase/decrease in cash" = Operating + Investing + Financing activities total. Check that it reconciles: Opening Cash + Net Change = Closing Cash.
