  Bharatanatyam:
    State: Tamil Nadu
    Origin: Devadasi temple tradition
    Governing text: Natya Shastra
    Characteristic: Pure dance (Nritta) + expressive
      (Abhinaya) combination

  Kathak:
    State: Uttar Pradesh / North India
    Origin: Storytelling tradition (Kathakas = storytellers)
    Characteristic: Fast footwork (tatkar), spins (chakkar)
    Two gharanas: Lucknow (lyrical) and Jaipur (vigorous)

  Kathakali:
    State: Kerala
    Characteristic: Elaborate makeup, costume, facial
      expressions — tells stories from Ramayana/Mahabharata
    Performers: Traditionally only male

  Mohiniyattam:
    State: Kerala
    Name meaning: Dance of the enchantress (Mohini)
    Characteristic: Soft, graceful feminine movements
    Costume: White and gold saree

  Kuchipudi:
    State: Andhra Pradesh / Telangana
    Origin: Village of Kuchipudi in Krishna district
    Characteristic: Mix of dance and drama (dance-drama)
    Distinctive: Dancers perform on brass plate
      (Tarangam) as finale

  Odissi:
    State: Odisha
    Origin: Temples of Odisha (Jagannath cult)
    Characteristic: Tribhangi posture
      (three-body bend: head, torso, legs)

  Manipuri:
    State: Manipur
    Characteristic: Soft, subtle movements — no
      stamping of feet (unlike Bharatanatyam/Kathak)
    Known for: Ras Lila dances depicting Radha-Krishna

  Sattriya:
    State: Assam
    Origin: Vaishnavite monasteries (Sattras)
    Founded by: Srimanta Sankardeva (15th century)
    Recognition: Declared classical in 2000 (latest to join)
