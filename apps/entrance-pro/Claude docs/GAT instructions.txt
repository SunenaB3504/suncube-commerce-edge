## DISTRACTOR QUALITY STANDARD — GAT EDITION

CUET GAT uses "Calculation Echo" distractors.
Every CUET question must follow this rule:

  Option 1: Correct answer
  Option 2: Answer from most common mistake at Step 2
  Option 3: Answer from most common mistake at Step 1
             (or formula misremembered)
  Option 4: Answer that looks "round" and feels right
             (psychological anchor trap)

Example — Successive Discount question:
  MP = ₹800, discounts = 50% + 50%
  Option 1: ₹200   ← Correct (800 × 0.5 × 0.5)
  Option 2: ₹400   ← Only first discount applied
  Option 3: ₹0     ← "50+50=100% off" linguistic trap
  Option 4: ₹320   ← 800 × 0.6 × 0.67 (wrong formula attempt)

This distractor architecture must be applied to all
quantitative and reasoning questions in CUET sections.
