UNIT 1 - Part 1: National Income Core [🔥 CRITICAL]

12.75 Marks

📖 Circular Flow & Aggregates

  • Generation Phase: Value Added (Primary, Secondary, Tertiary sectors).
  • Distribution Phase: Payments (CoE, OS, MI).
  • Disposition Phase: Expenditure (C + G + I + X-M).
  • GDP Deflator: (Nominal / Real) × 100. Measures price level % change.

🎯 Flow vs Stock Traps

  • FLOW: Measured over a period of time (Investment, Income, Depreciation).
  • STOCK: Measured at a specific point in time (Wealth, Capital, Money Supply).

🚨 GDP & Welfare Exception

  • Externalities: Positive (public parks) or negative (pollution) effects not in GDP.
  • Non-monetary Exch: Barter systems understate the actual GDP welfare.

🎯 NI Examiner Traps

  • Machine Trap: If a firm buys a machine, it is Final/Investment, NOT Intermediate count.
  • Pension Trap: Old Age = Transfer (Exclude). Retirement = Factor Income (Include).
  • Inventory Trap: Change in stock is investment even if unsold.

🧮 Conversion Rules Ecosystem

GROSS to NET = Subtract Depreciation (Consumption of Fixed Capital) DOMESTIC to NATIONAL = Add NFIA (Factor Income from Abroad - Paid to Abroad) MARKET PRICE (MP) to FACTOR COST (FC) = Subtract NIT (Indirect Taxes - Subsidies)

UNIT 1 - Part 2: Measurements [🔥 CRITICAL]

📊 Measurement Methods

  • 1. Value Added Method:
    GVA(MP) = Value of Output - Intermediate Consumption.
    Yields: GDP(MP)
  • 2. Income Method:
    CoE + Operating Surplus + Mixed Income.
    Yields: NDP(FC) (Domestic Income)
  • 3. Expenditure Method:
    PFCE + GFCE + GDCF (Fixed Cap + Change in Stock) + Net Exports.
    Yields: GDP(MP)

🔍 Precautions during Measurement

  • Value Added: Do NOT include sale of second-hand goods, but DO include commission earned on them.
  • Income: Exclude transfer payments (gifts, subsidies). Include imputed rent of owner-occupied houses.
  • Expenditure: Exclude expenditure on shares/bonds (financial assets, not production).
  • Windfall gains: Lotteries are abruptly excluded from National Income.

🔤 Essential Abbreviations

GDP/GNP: Gross Domestic/National Product
NDP/NNP: Net Domestic/National Product
NFIA: Net Factor Income from Abroad
NIT: Net Indirect Taxes (Ind. Taxes - Subsidies)
CFC: Consumption of Fixed Capital (Depreciation)

🚨 Capital Loss vs Depreciation

  • Depreciation: Expected wear/tear. Part of production.
  • Capital Loss: Unforeseen (Natural Calamity, Fire). Not part of Depreciation.

UNIT 2 - Part 1: Money & Credit [🟡 HIGH YIELD]

10.25 Marks

💰 Functions & Supply of Money

  • Primary: Medium of Exchange (solves double coincidence of wants), Measure of Value.
  • M1: C + DD + Other deposits with RBI. M3: M1 + Net Time Deposits. NDTL: Net Demand and Time Liabilities.
  • High Powered Money (H): C (with public) + R (Reserves with banks). The base of the economy.

🏦 Commercial Banks & Credit Creation Process

Banks create credit mathematically based on initial deposits and the legal reserve ratio.

  • Two Assumptions: All banks are treated as a single unit. All transactions are routed through banks.
  • Process: Banks keep a fraction (LRR) of deposits and lend the rest. The loan is deposited back into the banking system, creating secondary deposits.
Credit Multiplier = 1 / LRR Total Credit = Initial Deposit × Multiplier

🎯 SQP Traps: Money & Banking

The Inverse Relation Trap: Higher Legal Reserve Ratio (LRR/CRR) logically means Lower Credit Creation, because Multiplier = 1 / LRR.

The Coin Trap: RBI does NOT issue ₹1 notes or coins. The Ministry of Finance does.

UNIT 2 - Part 2: Reserve Bank of India

👑 Functions of Central Bank (RBI)

  • Currency Authority: Sole right to issue fiat money. Uniformity.
  • Banker to Gov: Manages govt accounts, acts as advisor.
  • Banker's Bank: Keeps reserves of commercial banks.
  • Lender of Last Resort: Rescues banks facing bankruptcy.

🎛️ Quantitative Tools

Controls TOTAL volume of credit.

  • Repo Rate: Rate RBI lends to banks. (Increase = Less Money).
  • OMO (Open Market Ops): RBI Sale = Absorbs cash.
  • LRR (CRR + SLR): Defines legal minimum reserves.

🎯 Qualitative Tools

Controls FLOW/DIRECTION of credit.

  • Margin Requirement: Diff between loan amount & security value.
  • Moral Suasion: Advice to banks.
  • Selective Credit Control: Quotas for specific sectors.

🔤 Essential Abbreviations

LRR: Legal Reserve Ratio (Minimum reserves banks must keep)
CRR: Cash Reserve Ratio (Fraction of deposits kept with RBI)
SLR: Statutory Liquidity Ratio (Fraction kept with themselves)
OMO: Open Market Operations (Buying/selling govt securities)

🚨 SQP Examiner Traps

  • The Coin Trap: RBI does NOT issue ₹1 notes or coins. The Ministry of Finance does.
  • Interest Rate Inverse Trap: Bond prices and Interest rates move in opposite directions (i ↑, Bond Price ↓).
  • CBDC Vulnerabilities: Purely digital CBDC introduces cyber vulnerabilities requiring robust preparedness.
  • Routine Inspections: Part of the RBI's Supervisory/Regulatory body function.

UNIT 3 - Part 1: AD, AS & Equilibrium [🔥 CRITICAL]

12.00 Marks

📈 Aggregate Demand & Supply

  • AD (AD = C + I): Total planned expenditure.
  • C-Function: C = c̅ + bY. (c̅ is autonomous, b is MPC).
  • AS (AS = Y = C + S): Total planned production (National Income).
  • S-Function: S = -c̅ + (1-b)Y.

Propensities

  • APC = C/Y. Can be > 1.
  • APS = S/Y. Can be negative.
  • MPC (b) = ∆C/∆Y.
  • MPS (1-b) = ∆S/∆Y.
APC + APS = 1 (Income = C + S) | MPC + MPS = 1

⚖️ Equilibrium Mechanics

Equilibrium occurs where planned AD = planned AS.

  • Equilibrium: AD = AS (45° line) | S = I Appr.
  • Effective Demand: Level of AD where it is exactly met by AS. All output is sold.
  • Mechanism (AD > AS): Stocks fall → Firms increase output/employment.
  • Mechanism (AS > AD): Stocks rise → Firms cut output/employment.
  • Break-even: Level where C = Y and S = 0 (APC = 1).

UNIT 3 - Part 2: Multiplier & Deficient Demand

⚙️ Investment Multiplier (k)

k = ∆Y / ∆I k = 1 / (1 - MPC) k = 1 / MPS
  • Direct relationship with MPC.
  • Inverse relationship with MPS.
  • Min value is 1 (MPC=0). Max is infinity (MPC=1).

🚨 Paradox of Thrift Trap

If everyone decides to save more (Thrift), AD falls. This leads to lower production and ultimately the Total Savings fall because total income crashed!
Key: Individual virtue can be a collective disaster.

📉 Deficient vs Excess Demand Remedies

  • Deficient (Deflationary): AD < AS at full employment.
  • Fiscal: Increase Govt Spending, Decrease Taxes.
  • Monetary: Decrease Repo, Decrease CRR, Buy bonds.
  • Excess (Inflationary): AD > AS at full employment.
  • Fiscal: Decrease Govt Spending, Increase Taxes.
  • Monetary: Increase Repo, Increase CRR, Sell bonds.

🔤 Essential Abbreviations

AD/AS: Aggregate Demand / Aggregate Supply
APC/APS: Average Propensity to Consume / Save
MPC/MPS: Marginal Propensity to Consume / Save
c̅: Autonomous Consumption (Survival consumption at zero income)

UNIT 4 - Part 1: Government Budget [🟡 HIGH YIELD]

9.00 Marks

📜 Budget Concepts & Objectives

  • Public Goods: Non-rivalrous & Non-excludable (Streetlights, Defence). Suffers from Free Rider Problem.
  • Sin Taxes: Heavy taxes on alcohol/tobacco (Allocation Function).
  • Redistribution: Progressive taxation (Equity). Stability: Managing AD via budget moves.

💸 Receipts

  • Revenue: Neither creates liability nor reduces asset. (Taxes, Fees, Fines).
  • Capital: Creates liability (Borrowings) OR reduces asset (Disinvestment, Recovery of Loans).

💳 Expenditures

  • Revenue: No asset created, no liability reduced. (Salaries, Pensions, Subsidies).
  • Capital: Creates asset (Building roads, shares) OR reduces liability (Repaying loan).

UNIT 4 - Part 2: Deficits & Taxes

🔥 The Three Deficits

  • 1. Revenue Deficit: Rev Exp - Rev Receipts. Implies govt cannot meet its day-to-day regular expenses.
  • 2. Fiscal Deficit: Total Exp - Total Receipts (excl borrowings). It rigidly is identical to Total Borrowing Requirements.
Fiscal = Rev Deficit + Cap Exp - Non-debt Cap Receipts

3. Primary Deficit (Sins of the Past Trap): Measures current year's overspending purely without historical debt baggage.
Formula = Fiscal Deficit - Interest Payments.

⚖️ Tax Structure

  • Direct Taxes: Burden cannot be shifted. Imposed directly on income/wealth of individuals. (e.g., Income Tax, Corporate Tax).
  • Indirect Taxes: Burden can be brutally shifted. Imposed on goods and services. (e.g., GST, Customs Duty).

🔤 Essential Abbreviations

PSU: Public Sector Undertaking
GST: Goods and Services Tax (Key Indirect Tax)
Rev. Deficit: Revenue Deficit (Revenue Exp - Revenue Receipts)
Fiscal Deficit: Total Borrowing Requirements

🎯 SQP Examiner Traps

  • Deficit Drivers: When citing case studies, aggressively extract terms like "astronomical tax collections", "massive dividends", and "restrained revenue expenditure".
  • Allocation vs Redistribution: Subsidising beneficial Khadi/solar projects optimally allocates/redirects resources. The "Robin Hood" approach redistributes income equitably.
  • Autonomous vs Accommodating: Profit motive (Autonomous) vs Gap-filling (Accommodating - ORT).
  • Primary Deficit = 0: Means borrowing only to pay interest (Sins of the past).
  • PMGKAY (Food Scheme): Represents Redistribution of Income objective.

UNIT 5 - Part 1: Balance of Payments

5.50 Marks

🌍 Balance of Payments Accounts

A systematic record of all economic transactions between residents of a country and the rest of the world.

  • Current Account: Export/Import of Goods (Visible Trade/BoT), Services (Invisible), Current transfers (Unilateral). Does not impact asset/liability status.
  • Capital Account: FDI, FPI, Borrowings (Commercial, External Assistance), Official Reserve Transactions. Impacts asset/liability status.
  • Autonomous Items: Undertaken for profit motive (Above the line). Cause of BoP imbalance.
  • Accommodating Items: Undertaken strictly to cover BoP deficit (Below the line). E.g. utilizing Forex reserves.

🚢 Balance of Trade vs BoP

  • BoT: Difference between Export and Import of Goods only. Narrow concept.
  • BoP: Much broader concept entirely encompassing Goods, Services, and Capital flows.

UNIT 5 - Part 2: Foreign Exchange

💱 Foreign Exchange Rate Systems

  • NEER: Trade-weighted average of nominal exchange rates.
  • REER: NEER adjusted for inflation differences (Competitiveness indicator).
  • Autonomous Items: Profit-driven (Above the line). Cause of imbalance.
  • Accommodating Items: Gap-filling (Below the line). Utilizing reserves.

🎯 Currency "Apple" Rule Traps

Always treat the US Dollar ($) like an Apple. If price moves ₹70 → ₹80, Apples get more expensive, meaning ₹ value has fallen.

  • Depreciation (Market): ₹70 → ₹85. Rupee weaker. Exports cheaper (Benefit).
  • Appreciation (Market): ₹70 → ₹60. Rupee stronger. Imports cheaper.
  • Devaluation: Deliberate fall in ₹ value by Govt (Fixed system).
  • REER: Real Effective Exchange Rate. Adjusted for inflation. The true indicator of Competitiveness.

🔤 Essential Abbreviations

BoP: Balance of Payments (Overall record of transactions)
BoT: Balance of Trade (Only visible goods)
FDI/FPI: Foreign Direct / Portfolio Investment
Forex: Foreign Exchange (Foreign currency reserves)

UNIT 6 - Part 2: Economic Reforms 1991

🚪 The 1991 LPG Reforms

Triggered by severe BoP crisis, high inflation, and rapidly dwindling forex reserves (barely 2 weeks of import cover).

  • Liberalisation: Abolishing industrial licensing (except 5 hazardous ones), financial sector deregulation, tax slab reductions.
  • Privatisation: Disinvestment (sale of equity of PSUs). Improving efficiency via Maharatna/Navratna status.
  • Globalisation: Integration with world economy. Reducing tariffs, dismantling quotas, increasing FDI limits, devaluation of Rupee.
  • Outsourcing: India emerged as a BPO destination (Cheap skilled labor, English).

🎯 SQP Traps & Organizations

  • WTO (1995): Successor to GATT. Rules-based trading regime. India is a founding member.
  • Demonetization (2016): Aimed to definitively curb black money, fake currency, and promote digital transactions.

🔥 Chronology Master Trap: Great Leap Forward (1958) → China Reforms (1978) → Pakistan Reforms (1988) → India Reforms (1991) → WTO (1995) → Demonetization (2016) → GST (2017).

🔤 Essential Abbreviations

LPG: Liberalisation, Privatisation, Globalisation (1991)

🚨 SQP Specific Highlights

  • License Raj & Equality: Licenses forcefully pushed factories to deeply poor countryside areas via tax holidays, attempting Regional Equality.
